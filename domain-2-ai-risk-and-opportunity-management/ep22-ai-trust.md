# AI Trust Explained

This opening episode of Domain 2 in the **ISACA Advanced in AI Security Management (AAISM)** exam prep series explores why AI trust sits at the very heart of AI risk. It explains how the harms an AI system can cause stretch far beyond the technical, and walks through the specific attributes that let an organization actually demonstrate its AI can be trusted. By the end you will be able to evaluate a tool not just on accuracy, but on fairness, safety, and transparency — exactly what regulators and users increasingly demand.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why is trust considered the heart of AI risk?
Trust is the currency of every AI system. Once it is lost, the system gets abandoned no matter how clever it is. Because AI systems learn, adapt, and evolve in ways nobody fully predicted, their risk reaches far beyond technical failure into ethics, fairness, privacy, security, reputation, and the wider welfare of society.

### Why does AI risk go beyond technical failure?
AI tries to mimic human behavior within a messy web of social factors, so its risk touches ethics, fairness, privacy, security, reputation, and the welfare of society. A purely technical view of AI risk will always miss the bigger picture, which is why trust must be managed through a risk-based approach.

### What are the attributes of trustworthy AI?
Trustworthy AI must be **valid and reliable**, **safe**, **secure and resilient**, **accountable and transparent**, **explainable and interpretable**, **privacy-enhanced**, and **fair** with harmful bias actively managed. No single attribute is enough on its own. Trust comes from holding all of them together.

### What happens when AI trust is not actively managed?
When trust is not actively managed, AI can rapidly introduce harmful bias and erode confidence in the system. This is precisely why structured risk management frameworks for AI have emerged in recent years, ensuring AI is deliberately built to support the welfare of whoever uses it.
